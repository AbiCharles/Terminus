Add a `stats` subcommand: `/app/quake stats [filters] --out PATH` accepts the same filters as `query`, computes recurrence statistics on the filtered events, and writes a JSON summary to `PATH` (or to standard output if `--out` is omitted).

Bin magnitudes with a fixed width `delta_m = 0.1`; an event of magnitude `m` falls in the bin whose center is `floor(m / delta_m + 0.5) * delta_m`. Estimate the magnitude of completeness `mc` with the Wiemer & Wyss (2000) goodness-of-fit test by scanning candidate completeness values from low to high, exactly as follows:

1. The candidate completeness values are the magnitude bin centers from the lowest filled bin up to (and including) the highest bin that still leaves at least four bins — counting both filled and empty bins — at or above it. Evaluate the candidates in increasing magnitude order.
2. A candidate `Mc` is admissible only if at least 30 events lie in bins at or above it. Skip inadmissible candidates entirely (do not let them be selected and do not consider them for the fallback below).
3. For an admissible candidate with `N` events at or above it and mean magnitude `mean` over those `N` events, fit the Aki-Utsu model: `b = log10(e) / (mean - (Mc - delta_m/2))` and `a = log10(N) + b*Mc`.
4. Compute the goodness-of-fit residual `R = 100 * (1 - D / S)`. Here `D` and `S` are **sums** taken over every bin center `M` from `Mc` up to the highest filled bin — one term per bin — where `B(M)` is the observed cumulative count (the number of events in bins whose center is ≥ `M`): `D = Σ |B(M) - 10^(a - b*M)|` and `S = Σ B(M)`. `R` is one minus the *total* absolute residual divided by the *total* observed count; it is an aggregate over all bins at or above `Mc`, never a single worst-bin (maximum) deviation.
5. Choose `mc` as the lowest admissible candidate whose `R` is at least 90. If no admissible candidate reaches 90, choose the admissible candidate with the highest `R`.

Report the final b-value from the Aki-Utsu estimator above applied to the events at or above `mc`, its Shi & Bolt (1982) uncertainty `sigma_b = 2.30 * b^2 * sqrt( Σ (m_i - mean)^2 / (N*(N-1)) )` (the sum over those same `N` events), and `a = log10(N) + b*mc`.

Write JSON with these keys: `completeness_method` (the string `goodness_of_fit`), `gft_confidence` (90.0), `delta_m` (0.1), `n_total` (events after filtering), `n_above_mc` (events at or above `mc`), `mc`, `b_value`, `b_uncertainty`, `a_value`, `catalog_years` (the span from earliest to latest filtered event time in years, i.e. the difference of Julian days divided by 365.25), and `magnitude_range` (an object with `min` and `max`). Use one digit after the decimal point for `mc`, `delta_m` and the magnitude range, and four digits for `b_value`, `b_uncertainty`, `a_value` and `catalog_years`.

The estimate requires the filtered set to span at least 4 magnitude bins and to contain at least one admissible candidate (so the chosen `mc` always leaves at least 30 events at or above it); when the filtered catalog is too small to satisfy this — including the case of no matching events — exit with status code `3`. Invalid invocations exit `2` exactly as for `query`; success exits `0`.
