Add a `stats` subcommand: `/app/quake stats [filters] --out PATH` accepts the same filters as `query`, computes recurrence statistics on the filtered events, and writes a JSON summary to `PATH` (or to standard output if `--out` is omitted).

Bin magnitudes with a fixed width `delta_m = 0.1`; an event of magnitude `m` falls in the bin whose center is `floor(m / delta_m + 0.5) * delta_m`. Estimate the magnitude of completeness `mc` with the Wiemer & Wyss (2000) goodness-of-fit test: for each candidate completeness equal to a bin center, take the events in bins at or above it, fit a Gutenberg-Richter model to that sample, and measure how well it reproduces the observed cumulative counts. Concretely, for a candidate `Mc` with sample size `N` and mean magnitude `mean`, the Aki-Utsu b-value is `b = log10(e) / (mean - (Mc - delta_m/2))` and `a = log10(N) + b*Mc`. For every bin center `M` from `Mc` up to the maximum filled bin, compare the observed cumulative count `B(M)` (events in bins with center ≥ `M`) to the predicted `10^(a - b*M)`, and define the goodness-of-fit residual `R = 100 * (1 - sum|B(M) - 10^(a - b*M)| / sum B(M))`. Choose `mc` as the lowest candidate whose `R` is at least 90; if no candidate reaches 90, choose the candidate with the highest `R`.

Report the final b-value from the Aki-Utsu estimator above applied to the events at or above `mc`, its Shi & Bolt (1982) uncertainty `sigma_b = 2.30 * b^2 * sqrt( sum((m_i - mean)^2) / (N*(N-1)) )` over those same `N` events, and `a = log10(N) + b*mc`.

Write JSON with these keys: `completeness_method` (the string `goodness_of_fit`), `gft_confidence` (90.0), `delta_m` (0.1), `n_total` (events after filtering), `n_above_mc` (events at or above `mc`), `mc`, `b_value`, `b_uncertainty`, `a_value`, `catalog_years` (the span from earliest to latest filtered event time in years, i.e. the difference of Julian days divided by 365.25), and `magnitude_range` (an object with `min` and `max`). Use one digit after the decimal point for `mc`, `delta_m` and the magnitude range, and four digits for `b_value`, `b_uncertainty`, `a_value` and `catalog_years`.

A candidate completeness is only admissible if its sample has at least 30 events. The estimate requires the filtered set to span at least 4 magnitude bins and to leave at least 30 events at or above `mc`; when the filtered catalog is too small to satisfy this (including the case of no matching events), exit with status code `3`. Invalid invocations exit `2` exactly as for `query`; success exits `0`.
